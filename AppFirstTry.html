Butterflies are some of the Longest tenured ambassadors of nature. The simple beauty and delicacy of a butterfly has served to remind us to stop and smell the roses probably for as long as there have been roses to smell! verse. The butterfly walk is intended to be taken at your own pace and hopefully provides the flexibility to talk with any of the ambassadors along the way. Each button takes you to a different highlight of the path. We do not provide video because after all the tour is around you not in your hand. Each button sends you to a small podcast - these are updated and will change from time to time to keep things fresh. In the menu there is an identification page with pictures Taken here at Johns Pass. There is also a link to Butterflies and Moths of North America (BAMONA) detailing known species in Pinellas county florida Also in the menu there is a quick link to your camera so that if there is a butterfly holding still you can interupt everything to quick grab a photo! We maintain our own data on butterflies here at johns pass and we support the larger effort of BAMONA. We welcome and encourage citizen science types to grab a picture and add it to the database (there is a button on the menu for this! The tour takes about an hour and encompasses about a one mile full trip walk. Start button First stop Clock wise button First stop counter clock wise button Display of all buttons
